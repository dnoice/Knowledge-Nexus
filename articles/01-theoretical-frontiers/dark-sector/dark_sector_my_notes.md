## üí° Personal Observations & Synthesis

### What I Find Most Compelling

**The Dark Dimension Scenario:**
This is the most elegant theoretical framework I've encountered. It takes seemingly unrelated facts:

- Dark energy is tiny
- Dark matter exists
- String theory has extra dimensions

And weaves them into a single prediction: DM = KK gravitons in a micron-scale extra dimension. The fact that this ALSO predicts evolving dark energy (which DESI may be seeing) is remarkable.

**The Intensity Frontier Pivot:**
For decades, "high energy physics" meant higher and higher collision energies. The realization that new physics could be light but feebly coupled is a genuine paradigm shift. It explains why the LHC hasn't found BSM physics AND opens entirely new experimental approaches.

### What I'm Most Skeptical Of

**Too Many Free Parameters:**
Rich dark sectors can explain almost anything because they have so many adjustable parameters. This makes them hard to falsify. The field needs to identify SPECIFIC predictions that MUST hold for particular models.

**Darkogenesis Gravitational Waves:**
The interpretation of PTA signals as dark phase transitions is exciting but speculative. There are astrophysical explanations (supermassive black hole mergers) that need to be ruled out first.
